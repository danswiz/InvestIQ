## Target Property Types for Hall Conversion

When speaking with brokers, specifically request properties in these categories:

2. **Former Retail Big-Box Stores** - Large open spaces, existing parking lots, good accessibility
3. **School Buildings (Vacant)** - Built for assembly, often have large halls, parking
4. **Church Buildings** - Designed for assembly, may have fellowship halls
5. **Community Centers** - Purpose-built for events and gatherings
6. **Banquet Halls (Existing)** - May need renovation but already zoned appropriately
7. **Gymnasiums/Sports Facilities** - Large open spaces with high ceilings
8. **Former Movie Theaters** - Large open spaces with parking

### Key Search Terms for Brokers:
- "Large floor plate" (15,000+ sq ft)
- "High ceilings" (12+ feet)
- "Ample parking" (75-100 spaces minimum)
- "Freestanding building"
- "Warehouse with office"
- "Flex space"
- "Special purpose property"

## Zoning Considerations for Hall/Event Venue

**Important:** Verify with the City of Framingham Planning Department:
- **Zoning District:** B (Business), I (Industrial), or MUB (Mixed-Use Business) typically allow assembly uses
- **Parking Requirements:** Usually 1 space per 3-5 seats for assembly halls
- **Occupancy Limits:** Fire marshal will determine maximum occupancy based on egress and square footage
- **Special Permits:** May need special permit for entertainment/assembly use
