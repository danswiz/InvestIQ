# Executive Research Sources & Strategies

## Primary Research Sources

### Professional Profiles
- **LinkedIn**: Primary source for career history, mutual connections, activity
- **Company Website**: Executive team page, bio, responsibilities
- **Crunchbase**: For startup/tech executives (funding history, board roles)
- **Bloomberg Terminal/Profile**: For public company executives

### News & Media
- **Google News**: Recent company and executive mentions
- **Press Releases**: Company announcements, executive appointments
- **Industry Publications**: Trade publications in their sector
- **Business Media**: Forbes, Fortune, WSJ, FT for major executives

### Thought Leadership
- **Podcast Appearances**: Search Spotify, Apple Podcasts, YouTube
- **Conference Speaking**: Event websites (SaaStr, Web Summit, industry conferences)
- **Published Articles**: LinkedIn articles, Medium, company blog
- **Books**: Amazon, Goodreads for authored books

### Company Intelligence
- **Earnings Calls**: For public companies (Seeking Alpha, company investor relations)
- **SEC Filings**: 10-K, 10-Q, proxy statements
- **Glassdoor**: Employee perspectives on company culture and leadership
- **G2/Capterra**: For software companies (product reviews, positioning)

## Information Quality Hierarchy

### Tier 1 (Highly Reliable)
- Company official executive page
- LinkedIn profile (self-maintained)
- SEC filings and official documents
- Verified news sources (Reuters, Bloomberg)

### Tier 2 (Good Sources)
- Industry publications with quotes
- Podcast interviews
- Conference speaker bios
- Trade publication profiles

### Tier 3 (Contextual)
- Glassdoor reviews (aggregate patterns)
- Social media activity
- Third-party databases (ZoomInfo, Apollo)

## Red Flags to Note

- **Frequent job changes**: May indicate instability or difficult to work with
- **Recent role changes**: New executives may have different priorities/mandates
- **Company turmoil**: Layoffs, missed earnings, leadership changes
- **Limited digital presence**: Harder to research, may prefer privacy
- **Controversies**: Search "[Name] controversy" or "[Company] lawsuit"

## Research Time Allocation

- **Tier 1 sources (40%)**: Company website, LinkedIn, official bios
- **Tier 2 sources (30%)**: News, interviews, thought leadership
- **Tier 3 sources (20%)**: Industry context, company analysis
- **Network research (10%)**: Mutual connections, introduction paths

## Geographic Considerations

### US Executives
- SEC filings available for public companies
- Strong LinkedIn presence
- Conference circuit well-documented

### European Executives
- GDPR may limit online information
- Check local business registries
- Industry associations often more important

### APAC Executives
- WeChat/Weibo for China executives
- Limited English content for some regions
- Local business media important

## Data Freshness

- **Check dates on all information**
- Prioritize sources from last 6-12 months
- Note when executive started current role
- Flag any recent company events (acquisitions, IPOs)
