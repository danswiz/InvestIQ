**The Growth Engine:**
- Revenue growth accelerating? (Q/Q, Y/Y)
- Operating leverage improving? (Margins expanding)

- Recurring/Subscription revenue > 50%?

**Red Flags:**
- Customer concentration (>20% from one customer)
- Declining cohort retention
- Need constant heavy marketing spend for growth

### 3. üõ°Ô∏è MOAT DURABILITY

**Will the advantage last through the growth phase?**

**Moat Types Ranked by 10X Potential:**
1. **Network Effects** (strongest for 10x)
   - Each new user makes product more valuable
   - Switching costs compound over time
   - Examples: V, MSFT, META

2. **Ecosystem Lock-in**
   - Platform with developer/content ecosystem
   - High switching costs once integrated
   - Examples: NVDA CUDA, AAPL App Store

3. **Proprietary Technology/IP**
   - Patents + know-how + years of R&D
   - 2-3 year lead on competition minimum
   - Examples: ASML EUV, LLY GLP-1

4. **Scale + Brand**
   - Cost advantages that competitors can't match
   - Brand premium pricing
   - Examples: AMZN, HD

**10X Signals:**
- ‚úÖ Moat WIDENING as company grows
- ‚úÖ Multiple reinforcing moats
- ‚úÖ Competitors can't replicate even with capital
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Single moat type
- ‚ùå No moat (commodity product)

### 4. üë§ VISIONARY CEO / LEADERSHIP

**The multiplier effect of great leadership:**

**10X CEO Traits:**
- **Founder-led** (still has equity, skin in game)
- **Long-term vision** (10-20 year thinking, not quarterly)
- **Product-obsessed** (not just financial engineering)
- **Recruits elite talent** (A players hire A players)
- **Adapts to change** (pivots when needed, not stubborn)
- **Underpromises, overdelivers** (reliable execution)
- **Capital allocation discipline** (smart M&A, buybacks, R&D)

**Red Flags:**
- Professional CEO (hired gun, no equity)
- Excessive media focus (celebrity CEO)
- History of overpromising, underdelivering
- Related-party transactions / self-dealing
- High insider selling without explanation

**Examples:**
- Bezos (AMZN) - 20+ year horizon, customer obsession
- Huang (NVDA) - Bet company on CUDA, AI vision
- Musk (TSLA) - Visionary but execution inconsistent
- Sacks/Conway - Silicon Valley mafia, pattern recognition

### 5. üåä MARKET TAILWINDS

**Why is the market growing?**

**Secular vs Cyclical:**
- **Secular growth** = 10-20 year trend (demographics, technology shift)
- **Cyclical growth** = Economic cycle (reverses)

**10X Market Characteristics:**
- Market growing 20%+ CAGR
- Total Addressable Market (TAM) expanding
- Regulatory tailwinds (not headwinds)
- Cultural/societal shift supporting adoption
- Early innings (penetration < 20%)

**Current Mega-Trends Driving 10X Opportunities:**
1. **AI Infrastructure** - Data centers, chips, power
2. **GLP-1 Drugs** - Obesity/diabetes treatment explosion
3. **Electrification** - EVs, grid modernization, batteries
4. **Cybersecurity** - Zero trust, cloud security
5. **Space Economy** - Satellites, launch, defense
6. **Biotech** - CRISPR, cell therapy, AI drug discovery
7. **Reshoring** - Domestic manufacturing, automation
8. **Aging Demographics** - Healthcare, senior living

### 6. üéØ MEGA-TREND POSITIONING

**Is this a 1st Order or 2nd Order beneficiary?**

**1st Order = Direct Play (Higher Risk, Higher Reward)**
- The company CREATING the trend
- Examples: NVDA (AI chips), LLY (GLP-1 drugs), TSLA (EVs)
- **Pros:** Maximum upside if trend continues
- **Cons:** High valuations, execution risk, competition

**2nd Order = Infrastructure/Supplier (Lower Risk, Steady Growth)**
- The company ENABLING the trend
- Examples: VRT (data center power), TSM (chip foundry), PWR (grid infrastructure)
- **Pros:** Diversified customer base, essential infrastructure
- **Cons:** Lower margins, capital intensive

**3rd Order = Indirect Beneficiary (Speculative)**
- Companies that MIGHT benefit
- Examples: Office REITs (if AI creates jobs), random suppliers
- **Pros:** Cheap, contrarian
- **Cons:** Uncertain connection to trend

**10X Sweet Spot:**
- 1st Order with 2nd Order diversification
- OR pure-play 2nd Order with pricing power

## How to Use This Skill

1. **User asks:** "10x scan" or "hypergrowth analysis"
2. **You do:** 
   - Pull stocks from latest breakout scan (rater.py results)
   - Apply TenX framework to top 10-20 candidates
   - Score each on the 7 factors
   - Rank by 10X potential score
   - Write qualitative analysis for each
   - Email HTML report

3. **Key Output Sections:**
   - New Product/Innovation (what's driving growth)
   - Growth Engine (how fast, how durable)
   - Moat Analysis (will advantage last)
   - Leadership Assessment (CEO quality)
   - Market Tailwinds (why the market is growing)
   - Mega-Trend Position (1st vs 2nd order)
   - Why Now (timing catalysts)
   - 10X Score with justification

## Important Disclaimers

- 10X stocks are RARE (maybe 1-2 per year in entire market)
- Past 10X performers often don't repeat (TSLA post-2021)
- High risk = High reward (many 10X candidates go to zero)
- This is QUALITATIVE judgment, not quantitative proof
- Always pair with position sizing discipline
- Most "10X" predictions are wrong

**The goal isn't to find THE 10X stock. It's to find stocks with 10X CHARACTERISTICS and let probabilities play out.**
